01Creative direction

The night study

Phormula is the warm light on at midnight — the feeling of studying late and not being alone.

The rebrand is built on one image: a dark, quiet room and a warm lamp. The canvas is deep and calm — the darkness of focus, not of technology. Everything warm in the interface is knowledge behaving like light: the swirl, the flashcards, progress, and every call to action carry the ember gradient of the logo. Cold UI chrome recedes; warm content advances.

The flashcard is the atom of the brand. Alone it is a scrap of paper; in motion, thousands of them behave like a murmuration — scattered facts that organize into shapes you can recognize and keep. That single metaphor — pieces becoming pattern — drives the homepage story, the loading states, the empty states, and the study-mode iconography. Nothing moves on this site unless it is telling that story.

Tone: technological but human (hand-drawn mark, serif voice, handwritten annotations), trustworthy (restrained palette, generous space, no neon), premium (few elements, precisely finished), motion-led but calm (one hero moment per viewport, everything else quiet).

02Visual direction options

Three ways to be dark

All three respect the brief — dark-first, warm mark, no neon, no Discord clone. They differ in temperature and voice.

Option A

Lamplight

Warm & near
Amber-biased darkness, lamplight glows

Warmest read: browns beneath the black, amber dominates the gradient, cream text. Cosy and human.

Risk: drifts toward café-brand softness; the "technological" half of the brief fades. Hardest to keep premium at scale.

Option B

Observatory

Cool & vast
Indigo darkness, starfield, cards as constellations

Closest to your brainstorm video: deep space, faint stars, the warm mark as the only sun. Impressive and vast.

Risk: cool indigo + warm logo can feel like two brands; "vast" can read distant rather than supportive — the opposite of a study companion.

Option C — recommended

Ember & Ink

Deep & warm-neutral
Plum-black ink, ember light, serif voice

A near-black with a plum bias — dark leaning toward the logo's pink rather than away from it. Ember light is scarce and therefore precious. Serif narrative voice over a clean geometric UI face.

Why it wins: one temperature family from black to pink; scales from cinematic homepage to utilitarian dashboard without changing personality.

03Recommended direction

Ember & Ink — with Observatory's depth and Lamplight's warmth

The final direction is Option C as the structural system, borrowing deliberately from the other two: from Observatory, the sense of depth on the homepage only (parallax layers, sparse micro-stars at 2–3% opacity, blur-graded distance); from Lamplight, the emotional register of the copy and the glow treatment around primary actions. The dashboard uses none of the starfield — depth there comes from surface elevation, not space.

This document itself is rendered in the proposed system — the palette you are reading, the Fraunces/Manrope pairing, the button treatments, and the section rhythm are the recommendation.

S1 · The exhale

100–180vh · driven by scroll progress

Not a fade. The lockup breathes out: wordmark letters gain tracking (+0.06em per 10% progress), the swirl scales up 1→1.6 while its stroke thins and its opacity falls, tagline words drift apart on slight individual vectors, and a 0→6px blur ramps in. The whole group parallaxes upward at 0.4× scroll speed — it feels like moving through the mark, not past it. CTAs dock: they shrink and glide to the top-right corner, becoming the persistent nav actions.

S2 · The flock arrives

180–300vh · card count ramps 0 → ~1,800

Tiny flashcards emerge from the bottom-left, riding a diagonal current toward upper-right — a river with an S-bend, not a spray. Three depth layers (far: smaller, dimmer, 1px blur, 0.6× parallax; near: larger, brighter, 1.15×). Cards bank along their velocity like birds. Caption fades in at right: "Every subject starts as a thousand scattered pieces."

Deck colours, recalibrated for dark

Same seven slots users already know, re-tuned as luminous pastels that hold on ink surfaces (used as card spines, edges and tints — never full fills):

#6FC3FF · #FF7A85 · #7ED9B4 · #C9A6FF · #FFD966 · #FF8FC0 · #59D3DD

Usage rules

  • Text on gradient is always ink (#1A0F0A, 6.9:1). White on ember fails contrast (2.7:1) — never white-on-gradient.
  • Body text ≥ 4.5:1, large text and icons ≥ 3:1; text-low is never used for information.
  • Glow budget: at most one glowing element per viewport (hero mark, or primary CTA, or active study card). Glows are shadows of the gradient colours at ≤ 35% opacity, never filter: brightness.
  • Gradient appears only on: logo, primary CTA, progress fills, active-mode ring, selection highlights. Everything else uses flat ember.
  • Hover = surface up one step (ink-2→ink-3) + border up one step; active/pressed = scale .97, no colour change.
  • Deck colours never colour text; they mark ownership via 3px spines, dots and 12%-tint headers.

Responsive adaptation

  • Desktop/laptop: full experience; flock ≈ 1,800 cards, DPR capped at 2.
  • Tablet: flock ≈ 900, two depth layers, same story; storyboard scenes shorten to ~80vh each.
  • Mobile: flock ≈ 350 cards on a coarser grid (formations designed to read at 28×16 cells), captions become stacked scenes between formation moments; hero identical minus particle stars. If the device reports low memory or first-frame render > 24ms, swap to the pre-rendered poster sequence (still frames + crossfades) — concept preserved, cost near zero.
  • Touch: mode labels always visible; swipe study unchanged; hit targets ≥ 44px.

Accessibility commitments

  • prefers-reduced-motion: cinematic sequences → still-frame crossfades; UI transitions → 120ms opacity; flock canvas paused on a composed frame. (This document already honours it.)
  • All narrative copy is real DOM text (SEO + screen readers); canvas scenes carry aria-hidden with the story text adjacent.
  • Focus order follows the visual story; skip-link to main CTA; focus ring 2px ember on every interactive element.
  • Contrast per section 08; study "learned/not-learned" always pairs colour with icon + label.
  • Type minimums: 15px body, 12.5px captions; zoom to 200% without horizontal scroll.

14Risks & technical considerations

Where this can go wrong

Performance

  • 1,800 animated cards need a single instanced canvas/WebGL pass — never DOM nodes. Budget: < 8ms/frame mid-range laptop; measure on first frames and auto-drop density tiers.
  • Cap devicePixelRatio at 2; pause all canvases off-viewport (IntersectionObserver) and on tab blur.
  • Battery: idle animations stop after 30s without input on the hero.
  • LCP: hero text is DOM, canvas paints behind it; fonts subset (~180KB) with metric fallbacks to kill layout shift.

Design risks

  • Mask art quality decides the formations. Silhouettes must be commissioned/curated 2-tone illustrations; generated-looking art would undermine the whole page. Prototype "The First Desk" at 56×30 before committing.
  • Scroll narrative fatigue: keep total ≤ ~9 viewports; a persistent nav from S6 lets impatient visitors jump to CTA.
  • Dark-first email/receipt/OG surfaces need their own pass — warm mark on ink also has to work in light contexts (favicons, app-store, social cards).
  • Pink-on-dark can drift "neon" if overused — enforce the glow budget and flat-ember rule.

Compatibility

  • Safari: 3D card flips need -webkit-backface-visibility care; test blur-heavy layers (expensive on iOS) — prefer pre-blurred sprites.
  • Reduced-data / save-data header → poster mode automatically.
  • Keyboard/scroll restoration on route return; formation state must be resumable at any scroll offset (pure function of progress, no timelines).

Migration

  • Existing users know the light dashboard — ship dark as default with a one-time "new look" note and an obvious theme toggle; keep the light palette maintained.
  • Deck colours change values but not identities — sets keep their assigned slot; verify contrast in both themes.
  • Roll out homepage separately from app re-theme (phases 3 vs 5–7) to de-risk review.
15Functionally unchanged

  • Creating, editing, importing, copying, moving, renaming and deleting sets, files and cards; Bulk editor; Auto-Flashcard generation logic.
  • Quiz and Swipe study logic, learned/not-learned flows, "save not learned", bookmarks.
  • Auth: Google OAuth, email flows, Turnstile verification, OAuth consent, waitlist mechanics.
  • Routes and navigation structure; Supabase data model and integrations; account/profile settings behaviour.
  • Deck-colour assignments (the seven slots and which set owns which).
  • Light theme availability (it becomes the secondary theme rather than the default).
